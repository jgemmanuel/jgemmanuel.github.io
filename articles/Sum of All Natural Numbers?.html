Sum of All Natural Numbers?

2021-01-28 – J G Emmanuel

A controversial and often misinterpreted result seen in Physics, 1+2+3+\ldots+\infty=-\frac{1}{12}.

Surely, this means that the sum of all natural numbers is -0.08\dot{3}, right? Wrong.

The result stems from the Euler-Riemann Zeta Function, \zeta(s), and itâ€™s analytic continuation at \zeta(-1). Such a result cannot be interpreted as the sum of all natural numbers.

\begin{align*} \zeta(s)&=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^s} \end{align*} \begin{align*} \zeta(-1)&=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^{-1}}\\ &=\frac{1}{1^{-1}}+\frac{1}{2^{-1}}+\frac{1}{3^{-1}}+\ldots\\ &=1+2+3+\ldots\\ &=-\frac{1}{12} \end{align*}
